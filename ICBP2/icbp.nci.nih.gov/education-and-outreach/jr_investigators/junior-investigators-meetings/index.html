Objectives and Goals of Junior Investigators Meetings

To enhance the training of junior investigators who are entering the field of cancer systems biology, the NCI – ICBP supports annual meetings for junior investigators associated with the ICBP program. These meetings focus on topics not included in the junior investigators’ formal training such as:

  1. Career development issues unique to inter-disciplinary scientists; multi-disciplinary approaches to science:
    • Philosophy of multi-disciplinary research
    • Building and managing an inter-disciplinary team
    • Shared experiences from successful investigators in multi-disciplinary fields
  2. Career options – academic, industry and government
  3. Group discussions to explore common interests, issues, approaches and new technical tools and analyses.
  4. An in-depth exploration of the science being conducted at the twelve ICBP Centers.
  5. Funding opportunities
    • Funding sources, types of grants
    • How to write/obtain K99/R00 and K22 grants

The meetings provide cross-fertilization of ideas about the diverse disciplines which are embedded in the field of cancer systems biology (e.g., cancer biology, computational modeling, data integration, engineering and mathematics) among the meeting participants who are the rising investigators in this field.

The ICBP intends the annual meetings will benefit the entire cancer systems biology research community as interactions at the meeting will facilitate the generation of a nucleus of junior investigators who will seed the future field of cancer systems biology. These training experiences prepare these investigators to obtain positions as independent investigators.
