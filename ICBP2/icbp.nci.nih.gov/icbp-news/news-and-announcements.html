Paper Highlight: Biomarker Identified for Predicting Early Prostate Cancer Aggressiveness
A team led by Cory Abate-Shen, Michael Shen, and Andrea Califano at Columbia University found that measuring the expression levels of three genes associated with aging can be used to predict the aggressiveness of seemingly low-risk prostate cancer.

Paper Highlight: Addressing genetic tumor heterogeneity through computationally predictive combination therapy
The Hemann and Lauffenburger labs used RNA interference in combination with computational modeling to design optimized drug regimens that yielded superior effects in the treatment of heterogeneous tumors.

Paper Highlight
In a recent paper published in Cancer Cell, ICBP investigators at the University of Texas Health Science Center, want to provide the potential mechanistic explanation how the known 20q13 and 17q23 amplicons transcriptionally control gene expression through long-range chromatin proximity. This paper describes how different large sets of data can be integrated for finding new biological mechanisms.

Robert Clarke Named as the National Cancer Institute - Sigma Xi Distinguished Lecturer for 2012-2013
